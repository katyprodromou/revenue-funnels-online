You're not here because you haven't put in the effort. You've tried. The problem is that most funnel advice is built for a different kind of business — and you've been trying to make it fit yours.

The problem
DIY funnels built from scattered advice.

The problem
Copywriters who didn't understand your offer.

They wrote something polished. It just didn't sound like you, didn't speak to your specific client, and didn't reflect why your approach is actually different. Generic copy produces generic results.

The problem
Tech that was set up but never tested.

Get In Touch

The things people tell me before they reach out…

And what I'd say back.

"The copy won't sound like me."

This is the number one thing people worry about — and honestly, it's a fair concern. You've probably seen the output when someone just hands their brand to a writer without any real immersion. It comes back sounding polished, generic, and completely disconnected from how you actually talk. That's not how I work. Before I write a single word, I go deep on your voice — your existing content, how you describe your clients' problems, the specific language you use. The copy I deliver sounds like you on your clearest, most confident day. Not like a stranger who read your About page once.

"I've already tried building a funnel. It didn't convert."

A funnel that doesn't convert usually has one of three problems: the strategy is wrong, the copy isn't specific enough, or the tech is broken. Most people patch one of those and hope for the best. What I build is all three, aligned, from the start. If you've already got something in place, we don't start from scratch unnecessarily — we diagnose what's failing and fix it. But we do fix it properly, not just tweak a headline and cross our fingers.

"My business is too relationship-based for a funnel."

I hear this a lot, and I understand why it feels true. You've built your reputation on real relationships, and the idea of automating any part of that feels clinical. But a funnel isn't a replacement for relationships — it's the thing that makes your relationships more efficient. Instead of manually warming up every single lead from zero, your funnel does that first layer of trust-building for you, so that by the time someone reaches you, they already feel like they know you. The relationship continues. You're just not doing all the heavy lifting alone anymore.

"I don't have time to be involved in a big project right now."

You don't need to be. That's the point of hiring someone. I handle the strategy, the writing, and the tech. What I need from you is a clear brief at the start, feedback at a couple of key checkpoints, and your approval before anything goes live. Most clients tell me the process takes significantly less of their time than they expected — and far less than they were spending trying to figure it out themselves.

"I'm not sure I have enough traffic to make a funnel worth it."

Traffic is a separate problem from conversion. A leaky funnel doesn't get better with more traffic — it just loses more people faster. Getting the structure right first means that when you do focus on driving more traffic, the infrastructure is ready to handle it. Most of my clients also find that fixing the funnel helps them get clearer on exactly who they're trying to attract — which makes the traffic problem easier to solve too.

"What if I invest in this and it still doesn't work?"

It's a real question, especially if you've spent money before and not seen results. What I can tell you is this: I don't take on projects I don't believe will move the needle. Before we agree on anything, I'll tell you honestly whether I think a funnel build is the right next step or whether something else needs to happen first. If the Revenue System Audit reveals a different bottleneck — your offer, your pricing, your positioning — I'll say so. I'd rather tell you that upfront than take your money and deliver something that can't perform.
